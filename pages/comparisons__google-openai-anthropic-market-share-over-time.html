Google vs OpenAI vs Anthropic market share over time

Summary

A true apples-to-apples company-wide AI market share metric is not available in the current wiki sources. The cleanest directly comparable time series is consumer AI chatbot usage share. On that measure, openai remains dominant through ChatGPT, while google's Gemini and anthropic's Claude have been gaining from much smaller bases.

For March 2026 worldwide chatbot share, the stack looks like this:

  • OpenAI / ChatGPT: 78.16%
  • Google / Gemini: 8.65%
  • Anthropic / Claude: 2.91%

A second lens is commercial traction rather than usage share. On disclosed annualized revenue, anthropic has recently narrowed or even overtaken openai despite much smaller chatbot usage share, which suggests enterprise coding and token-heavy workflows monetize differently from broad consumer traffic.

What this page is actually measuring

Primary metric

  • Worldwide AI chatbot usage share from Statcounter, monthly, 2025-04 to 2026-03.
  • This is the best over-time market-share series currently captured for a direct three-way comparison.

Secondary proxy metrics

  • Consumer scale: OpenAI's 900M+ weekly active users for ChatGPT and Google's 750M monthly users for the Gemini app.
  • Revenue scale: Reuters-reported annualized revenue comparison for OpenAI and Anthropic.

Important caveat

This page should be read as market share in the chatbot/application layer, not total AI industry share across cloud, model APIs, enterprise spend, chips, or search.

Net change across the captured period

  • OpenAI / ChatGPT: -6.05 percentage points from April 2025 to March 2026.
  • Google / Gemini: +6.34 percentage points over the same period.
  • Anthropic / Claude: +2.61 percentage points over the same period.

How to read the trend

OpenAI

  • openai remains overwhelmingly dominant in chatbot share.
  • But dominance at this layer does not mean uncontested momentum; share eased from 84.21% to 78.16% across the captured period.
  • OpenAI still looks like the consumer default, but rivals are taking some marginal share.

Google

  • google shows the clearest rise from the big-platform incumbents.
  • Gemini moved from roughly 2% share in spring 2025 to 8.65% by March 2026.
  • That matches the broader picture in google: Google is using product embedding and distribution across its ecosystem to convert platform reach into chatbot share.

Anthropic

  • anthropic remains much smaller at the consumer-chatbot layer.
  • But Claude's share rose from 0.30% to 2.91% over the captured period, with the steepest visible gain in early 2026.
  • The pattern is consistent with Anthropic being relatively stronger in specialized, work-oriented adoption than in pure mass-consumer distribution.

Revenue proxy: a different picture

Usage share and commercial power do not line up perfectly.

Why this matters

  • OpenAI wins decisively on chatbot usage share.
  • Anthropic may match or exceed OpenAI on revenue intensity in enterprise-heavy workflows.
  • Google has huge scale in users and infrastructure, but the current wiki sources do not provide a comparable standalone AI revenue series for Google the way Reuters does for OpenAI and Anthropic.

Bottom line

If the question is consumer chatbot market share over time, openai is still decisively ahead, google is the strongest challenger, and anthropic is growing from a smaller base.

If the question is who is winning commercially, the answer is more complicated. Anthropic's recent revenue trajectory suggests that smaller share at the chatbot layer can still translate into very large business outcomes when the workload mix is enterprise, coding, and token-intensive.
