OpenClaw

Summary

openclaw is an open-source personal AI assistant positioned around user-controlled infrastructure, persistent memory, chat-app access, and full-system/browser control on the user’s own machine.

Product framing

  • Marketed as personal rather than enterprise-first.
  • Emphasizes on-prem or user-controlled execution rather than a hosted workspace.
  • Supports a wide range of chat surfaces and model backends.

Strategic significance

openclaw is part of the open-agent wave around agentic-software-development. It suggests that some of the future of agentic AI may be controlled by users through hackable personal infrastructure, not only by frontier vendors through vertically integrated SaaS workspaces.

Related pages

  • hermes-agent shares the open, extensible, multi-surface direction.
  • codex and claude-code illustrate more vertically integrated frontier-lab approaches.
