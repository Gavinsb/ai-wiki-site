Google

Summary

Google frames AI policy around a three-part agenda of opportunity, responsibility, and security, emphasizing multi-stakeholder governance, international coordination, and continued innovation rather than halting development.

What this source adds

  • Google explicitly argues against broad pauses or halts in AI progress.
  • It presents governance as a combination of private-sector practices, industry standards, government policy, and international coordination.
  • It highlights public trust, economic competitiveness, workforce transition, and geopolitical security as simultaneous policy goals.

Themes in this source

  • innovation and competitiveness
  • responsible governance and risk reduction
  • international coordination
  • privacy and cross-border data flows
  • workforce upskilling and future-of-work research

Relationships

  • Google’s three-pillar framing resembles parts of industrial-policy-for-the-intelligence-age but is less centered on distributional industrial policy.
  • Its priorities are compared directly in anthropic-google-openai-us-government-ai-policy-framings.

Open questions
