Industrial Policy for the Intelligence Age

Summary

A policy framing, advanced by openai, that argues frontier AI and the move toward superintelligence require an updated industrial policy to distribute gains broadly, scale safety with capability, and preserve democratic agency.

Core claims from the source

  • AI is moving from automating tasks that take minutes to tasks that take hours and potentially months.
  • Market forces alone may not distribute benefits or respond fast enough to major capability transitions.
  • Industrial policy should combine research funding, workforce development, regulation, procurement, and investment.
  • Public-private experimentation and measurement should complement formal regulation.

Stated goals

  1. Share prosperity broadly
  2. Mitigate risks
  3. Democratize access and agency

Specific policy directions mentioned

  • Formal worker voice in AI deployment decisions
  • Energy and local-tax accountability for AI data centers
  • Institutional mechanisms that avoid regulatory capture
  • Pilots, grants, and public-facing policy iteration

Why it matters for the wiki

This is a useful umbrella page for AI political economy, compute infrastructure externalities, and labor/governance questions that sit adjacent to purely technical AI research.

Related pages

  • openai is the organization articulating this policy document.
  • superintelligence-governance covers the capability-governance premise behind the policy argument.

Open questions

  • Which parts of this agenda are unique to AI versus reapplications of classic industrial-policy tools?
  • What metrics would show whether prosperity, agency, and risk mitigation are actually being shared rather than merely promised?
