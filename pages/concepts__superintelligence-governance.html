Superintelligence Governance

Summary

A governance framing in which policymakers and institutions prepare for AI systems that could outperform the smartest humans even when those humans are assisted by AI. In this source, openai treats that transition as already beginning and argues that safety, distribution, and democratic legitimacy must scale alongside capability.

Claims in this source

  • The transition to superintelligence is framed as underway rather than hypothetical.
  • Safety must scale with capability.
  • Risks include misuse, loss of alignment or control, anti-democratic use, and concentration of wealth and power.
  • Governance is not only about preventing catastrophe; it is also about who gets agency, access, and economic upside.

Policy implications surfaced here

  • Governance should address labor, institutional adaptation, and wealth concentration, not just model misuse.
  • Democratic processes should have real influence over how advanced AI is deployed.
  • Industrial policy may become part of frontier-model governance, especially where compute, energy, and regional development are involved.

Related pages

  • industrial-policy-for-the-intelligence-age is one concrete policy response built on this framing.
  • openai is the actor advancing this particular argument in the source.

Open questions

  • What thresholds meaningfully distinguish frontier AI from superintelligence in governance practice?
  • Which governance mechanisms remain robust if capability progress is slower or less discontinuous than this paper assumes?
