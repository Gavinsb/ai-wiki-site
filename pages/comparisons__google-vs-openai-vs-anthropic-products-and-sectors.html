Google vs OpenAI vs Anthropic: products and sectors

Summary

google, openai, and anthropic are all competing for the center of the AI stack, but they are not attacking the market from identical starting points. Google is strongest where AI can be pushed through an existing mass-market product and cloud platform. OpenAI is pushing hardest for a unified consumer-plus-enterprise AI operating surface centered on ChatGPT and codex. Anthropic is building a more work-oriented family of products around high-trust delegation, especially through claude-code and claude-cowork.

The result is partial overlap rather than one single market. Some sectors are direct head-to-head battles, while others are asymmetric: Google has the biggest consumer distribution, OpenAI has the clearest superapp/platform thesis, and Anthropic has the most explicit delegated-work product segmentation.

Competing products

1. Consumer AI surfaces

  • Google: Gemini across Search, Maps, Workspace, Android/Pixel, and Chrome.
  • OpenAI: ChatGPT as the default consumer entry point into a broader platform.
  • Anthropic: Claude is present as a general assistant, but the strongest current differentiation in this wiki is around work execution rather than mass consumer embedding.

Takeaway: Google’s edge is distribution through existing products; OpenAI’s edge is having a single AI-first app identity. Anthropic is less dominant in this sector from the sources currently captured.

2. Coding and technical-work agents

  • Google: Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemini API, AI Studio, Android Studio, and Vertex AI form a broad developer surface.
  • OpenAI: codex is expanding from cloud coding agent toward a general-purpose technical-work system with computer use, browser workflows, plugins/MCP, memory, and automations.
  • Anthropic: claude-code is positioned as a project-level coding operator focused on verification, approvals, and reviewable autonomy.

Takeaway: OpenAI and Anthropic are the most clearly productized rivals here. Google competes more diffusely by embedding Gemini into its full developer ecosystem rather than by making one single coding-agent identity the center of the story.

3. Knowledge-work and workplace automation

  • Google: Gemini is pushed into Workspace and enterprise productivity surfaces.
  • OpenAI: OpenAI describes a superapp plus enterprise layer in which agents become part of company-wide workflows.
  • Anthropic: claude-cowork is the clearest direct product for delegated non-technical work across files, documents, and desktop tasks.

Takeaway: Anthropic is the most explicit about packaging non-technical delegated work as a named product. Google and OpenAI both compete here too, but through broader platform narratives.

4. Enterprise platform and infrastructure

  • Google: Vertex AI, Gemini Enterprise, Google Cloud, and custom hardware combine into a highly integrated stack.
  • OpenAI: OpenAI Frontier and the API/platform layer are meant to sit beneath enterprise agents and the superapp.
  • Anthropic: Enterprise Claude products sit on top of a multi-cloud strategy, but Anthropic remains more dependent on partners for infrastructure than Google.

Takeaway: Google has the strongest vertical integration. OpenAI is trying to become the default intelligence layer without owning the whole underlying cloud stack. Anthropic is using product quality, trust, and enterprise traction to compete despite less infrastructure ownership.

5. Open vs closed ecosystem reach

  • Google: google uses Gemma-style open releases to influence developers, edge use cases, and the wider ecosystem while keeping Gemini proprietary.
  • OpenAI: The current wiki evidence is centered more on proprietary platform expansion.
  • Anthropic: The current wiki evidence is also centered more on proprietary enterprise/work products.

Takeaway: Google is the clearest hybrid player across closed frontier models and selective open-model ecosystem strategy.

Strategic synthesis

Where they compete most directly

  • Google vs OpenAI: consumer AI default, enterprise AI platform, model/API platform, and overall mindshare.
  • OpenAI vs Anthropic: coding agents, delegated work, enterprise adoption, and trust in higher-autonomy workflows.
  • Google vs Anthropic: enterprise AI, infrastructure access, and distribution through cloud/model partnerships.

Where each company is relatively strongest

  • google: sectors where distribution, integration, and infrastructure scale matter most.
  • openai: sectors where one coherent AI operating surface can capture user habit, developer adoption, and enterprise workflow control.
  • anthropic: sectors where trust, reviewability, and domain-specific delegation matter more than mass consumer reach.

Most important asymmetry

This is not just a model-quality race. Google starts with distribution and infrastructure, OpenAI starts with product-layer centrality and consumer mindshare, and Anthropic starts with a higher-trust work-automation identity. That means the same market actually breaks into several overlapping sectors with different likely winners.
